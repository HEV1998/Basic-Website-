Brief history of the ragdoll

The (Ragdoll) cat is a relatively modern breed with an interesting origin story rooted in California during the 1960s. The breed was developed by a woman named Ann Baker, who sought to create a cat with a distinctive appearance and a notably gentle temperament. Ann Baker, a Persian cat breeder from Riverside, California, started the Ragdoll breeding program. The foundation of the Ragdoll breed began with a domestic longhaired white cat named Josephine, which belonged to Baker's neighbor. Josephine was believed to have had a mix of Persian and Angora in her lineage. After a car accident, Josephine exhibited unusual traits such as an exceptionally docile temperament and a tendency to go limp when held. Baker believed these characteristics were passed down to Josephines kittens.

Characteristics

Ragdolls are large, muscular cats with semi-long fur that is soft and silky. They typically have striking blue eyes and a color-pointed coat, which means their extremities (ears, face, paws, and tail) are darker than the rest of their bodies. Ragdolls are known for their calm, placid demeanor and affectionate nature. They are often described as “puppy-like” because they tend to follow their owners around and enjoy being handled and cuddled. Ragdolls generally have a good lifespan, ranging from 12 to 17 years. They are prone to certain health issues such as hypertrophic cardiomyopathy (HCM), but regular veterinary care can help manage these risks.

Fun facts

Docile Temperament

Ragdolls are known for their exceptionally gentle and calm demeanor. They often go limp when picked up, which is how they got their name, as they resemble a "rag doll."

Distinctive Appearance

Ragdolls have striking blue eyes and a color-pointed coat, meaning their ears, face, paws, and tail are darker than the rest of their body. They have semi-long, soft, and silky fur.

Loyal Companions

Ragdolls are known for their loyalty and affectionate nature. They enjoy being around their owners and are often described as "puppy-like" due to their desire for companionship.

Large Size

Ragdolls are one of the largest cat breeds Males typically weigh between 15-20 pounds (6.8-9.1 kg), while females are slightly smaller, weighing between 10-15 pounds (4.5-6.8 kg).

Health and Lifespan

Ragdolls generally have a good lifespan, averaging between 12-17 years. They are prone to certain health issues like hypertrophic cardiomyopathy (HCM), a common heart disease in cats, but with proper care, they can lead healthy lives.

Conclusion

Ann Baker’s efforts to create a new cat breed with desirable traits were successful, and the Ragdoll has become one of the most popular and beloved cat breeds worldwide. Its unique combination of beauty and gentle nature continues to endear it to cat lovers around the globe.
